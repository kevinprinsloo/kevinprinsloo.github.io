I'm a neuroscientist and psychologist interested in signal processing and machine learning applications in neuroscience and neurotechnology. Currently making the transition in industry as a data scientist. My research has focused on neural decoding, audiovisual speech processing, auditory attention, sensory deficits in RETT (CLN3) and Batten disease, speech recognition. Using neuroimaging technologies such as: magnetoencephalography (MEG), electroencephalography (EEG), intracranial electroencephalography (iEEG), and Eye-tracking,

Currently postdoctoral research associated at the University of Rochester in Biomedical Engineering and Neuroscience. My academic career has focused on trying to solve complex questions about how the brain processes information from the environment. Using data collected from state-of-the-art neuroimaging technologies my research has used various computational modeling techniques based on machine learning applications, signal processing and statistical analyses to investigate low-level acoustics processing to complex hierarchical language processing.
